    The main points that a draftsman can do are:-

    1. Drafting drawings based on an architect's design that will be used to construct buildings

    2. Using rough sketches, drawings and notes to create a design

    3. Preparing maps, wiring layouts, product design or machinery layouts

    4. Using different types of software to create a single design

    5. Revising and adjusting designs as needed during construction or product creation

    There are a wide variety of CAD drafter specialties, including architectural, engineering, electrical and civil drafting. Each of these requires a different set of skills and many of them require different software.

    What are important to learn in order to became a CAD DRAFTER?

    It is essential to learn the specific software for the type of drafting you will be doing. Organizations may be more willing to teach the engineering aspect of the job, but you are expected to be an expert in the software you will be using.

    There are different CAD drafter fields such as civil drafters, architectural drafters, and electrical drafters. While the work is similar, there are quite a few differences between CAD drafter fields. Civil drafters will likely be designing geographical maps, architectural drafters will be creating drawings of structural features and building designs, and electrical drafters create layout drawings and wiring diagrams. Other types of drafting designs might include creating the wiring layout for electronics or drawing a product design for manufacturers.

    A draftsman prepares technical drawings either manually or using computer-aided drafting. While CAD utilizes computer programs, manual drafting uses a myriad of tools that haven't changed much over the years. These include the T-Square, various straight edges, triangles, curves and erasing shields to keep projects clean. Many of the tools you've likely been introduced to in high school geometry, such as the compass and the protractor.

    Direct vs. Indirect Costs

    There are many factors that go into a construction project's cost estimate. They typically fall into one of two main categories: direct and indirect costs. Direct costs may include things like materials and labor, including wages for different parts of the team, equipment fuel and resources needed to get the job done.

    T-squares are used to draw straight lines. The head of the square, the cross member, is placed along the left edge or the top of the drafting table, while the square's blade is laid across the table's top, over the drawing paper. The drafter slides the square up, down or across on the tabletop, as required by a design, always keeping the flat side of the square flush with the tabletop's edge. This action keeps the square's blade parallel to the tabletop's edges. The drafter draws a horizontal line by resting the pencil's tip atop the square's blade, then pulling the pen right while maintaining a slight pressure against the blade.

    How to become a CAD Drafter

    CAD drafters are required to complete a two-year associate's degree after high school, usually at a community college or technical school. It is helpful to extend the education to a bachelor's degree but it's not necessary to get a job in the field. CAD drafters may also acquire an American Design Drafting Association (ADDA) certification to further legitimize their education
